Filipino Ancient Weapons Collection

Bolo

Warfare and self-defense played a crucial role in Filipino history, and the Filipino Ancient Weapons Collection at the Vesperium highlights the tools of battle used by warriors, revolutionaries, and freedom fighters. A centerpiece of this collection is the Bolo Knife, a multipurpose blade used in combat, agriculture, and daily tasks. Its curved, heavy blade made it a fearsome weapon during the Philippine Revolution and World War II, wielded by Katipuneros and guerrilla fighters alike.

Kampilan

Another significant weapon in the collection is the Kampilan, a long, single-edged sword favored by Muslim warriors and datu leaders in Mindanao. Often adorned with intricate carvings and decorated hilts, this weapon was known for its deadly efficiency in battle. Alongside it, the collection features the Bangkaw, a traditional Filipino spear used both for hunting and combat, often tipped with metal or hardened bamboo. The Panabas, a curved war sword from Mindanao, and the Kris, a wavy-bladed dagger symbolizing both spiritual and physical power, further showcase the evolution of Filipino weaponry.

These weapons tell the stories of heroism, survival, and resistance.
